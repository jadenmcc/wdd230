Overview

Purpose

I’m a website builder who also ran an online business selling high-end lightsabers to Star Wars fans.

Buying a lightsaber is like buying a car in two ways:

(1) Every manufacturer uses their own technical jargon to describe features that are universally offered. Before you can make accurate comparisons and determine which car is right for you, you have to translate their jargon into layman’s terms.

(2) If you want objective product reviews, your sources are often affiliate marketers who have financial incentive to bias you, or online forums where you have to sift through endless threads of questionable opinions.

Wouldn’t you have found your last car purchase easier if you had apples-to-apples comparisons and reviews, in layman’s terms, between makes/models? And all in one place?

That’s what my website does: consolidates information about lightsabers and translates it into plain English so people can make confident purchase decisions.

Audience

Star Wars fans: Men ages 18 to 45 or women ages 18 to 35, single or in a relationship; no dependents or children who are Star Wars fans ages 12 to 17 (male or female). Upper-lower class to upper class income range.

They likely attend one or more comic conventions per year, are technically oriented or craft oriented, participate in r/lightsabers or related Reddit forums, follow professional cosplayers on Instagram, and have bought or are intending to buy one or more lightsabers.

They love sharing in the fantasy of being a Force user.

Typography

Heading Font: Poppins

This is an informational website about high-end lightsabers. The overall branding should convey (1) a sense of connection to the Star Wars universe which lends to a feeling of credibility, (2) a sense of elegance which lends to a sense of authority about what standards should be around a potentially expensive toy, and (3) a sense of modernity which lends to a sense of being "up to date" or "cutting edge" on trends and style

I feel the Poppins font checks all of these boxes. Also, being sans-serif if helpful for screen reading.

Paragraph Font: Open Sans

(1) It checks all of the boxes for branding previously mentioned, (2) it's a good compliment for the heading font because they're both sans-serif fonts (good for screen reading) and will help convey hierarchy without being too different, and (3) it's pretty close to a few of the web safe fonts, so default settings will still be on brand.
